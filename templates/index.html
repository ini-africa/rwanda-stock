Why Invest in Bonds?

  • Regular Income: Bonds pay interest (coupons) at regular intervals, providing a steady cash flow.
  • Capital Preservation: Generally safer than stocks; you get your principal back at maturity.
  • Diversification: Adding bonds to your portfolio reduces overall risk.

Understanding ROI

Return on Investment (ROI) measures the gain or loss generated on an investment relative to the amount of money invested.

ROI = (Net Profit / Cost of Investment) x 100
